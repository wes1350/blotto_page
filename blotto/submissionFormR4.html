Blotto Fall 2018, Round 4: Defectors

Submissions due 5pm EST Tuesday, Nov. 20

Remaining soldiers: 100

General Blotto Rules:

  • Tournament structure is round-robin. Your entry will play every other entry once.
  • Your score for a round is your average score against the rest of the entries.
  • Win a field by having more soldiers on that field than your opponent.
  • You may submit multiple entries, but only your last one will count (make sure the names match).

Submission Rules (red = requirement not satisfied):

  • 6 fields, exactly 100 soldiers
  • Field entries must be nonnegative integers

Round Scoring:

  • Fields 1-6 are worth 5, 6, 7, 8, 9, 10, respectively
  • Tying on a field awards half the points to each player
  • Soldiers "defect" when outnumbered heavily! The fields are evaluated in order, left to right. If your opponent sends at least 2x as many soldiers to a field than you, they win that field and 1/2 (rounded down) of the soldiers you sent there defect to your opponent, adding to their soldier count on the next field. Does not apply to field 6.
  • As an example, if on field 2 you win 40:20, you will gain 10 soldiers for field 3.

Scoring Example:

Ripka submits [60, 20, 10, 0, 10, 0].
Willie submits [30, 10, 10, 10, 10, 30].

On field 1, Ripka wins, earning 5 points.
Also, since he has twice as many soldiers on field 1, half of Willie's 30 soldiers on field 1 now fight for Ripka on field 2.
On field 2, Ripka now has 35 soldiers. He again beats Willie, and earns 6 points.
Since he again had at least twice as many soldiers, half of Wilie's 10 soldiers on field 2 fight for Ripka on field 3.
On field 3, Ripka has 15 soldiers, and beats Willie, earning 7 points.
Since he did not send twice as many soldiers as Wilie, no soldiers of Willie's defect.
On field 4, Willie wins and gets 8 points. He doesn't gain any soldiers for field 5 though, since Ripka sent 0.
On field 5, they tie, and earn 4.5 points each.
On field 6, Willie wins outright and earns 10 points.
In total, Ripka's score is 5+6+7+4.5 = 22.5, and Willie's score is 8 + 4.5 + 10 = 22.5.
